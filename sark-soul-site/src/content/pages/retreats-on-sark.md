## What makes the house special?

The private lounge offers plenty of spaces for reflection and quiet corners for journaling or solitude. Unwind with a swim in the warm and inviting indoor pool, or experience tranquility in the heated outdoor pool, allowing the water to ease you back into a state of deep relaxation.

Stroll through the beautiful permaculture gardens, vegetable garden, cedar greenhouse, apple orchard, chicken pen, and bee hives. Nature and sustainability come together in award-winning gardens; discover the variety of plants and flowers that flourish here and the methods used to nurture these beautiful spaces.

Fresh, vibrant vegetarian food is prepared by our private chef using organic produce grown in our own garden. We also forage Dulse, wild Sark seaweed, prized for its natural truffle and black pepper notes. Nourishment is part of the healing.

Explore the island at your own pace by bike, discover scenic paths, hidden coves and breathtaking views, or experience Sark's traditional mode of transport with a scenic carriage ride.

## How does booking and travel work?

Most guests reserve their place first and organise travel with our support. Once your place is held, we will guide you through ferry timing, booking and any questions, so you don't need to have everything figured out before committing. A deposit holds your place, the rest unfolds with guidance.

**Step 1: Travel to Guernsey.** Guernsey is the main gateway to Sark. There are direct flights from the UK and Europe, including Aurigny, British Airways and Loganair. Allow around 20 to 30 minutes to travel from Guernsey Airport to the ferry by taxi or bus. You can also travel by ferry from the UK or France if you prefer a sea crossing.

**Step 2: Ferry from Guernsey to Sark.** From Guernsey, a [passenger ferry](https://sarkshipping.gg) takes you to Sark in around 55 minutes, with islands unfolding on the horizon. Ferry times vary by day, and we will share recommended sailings once your booking is confirmed.

**Step 3: Arrival on Sark.** Sark is car-free. On arrival, your luggage is taken care of, and you travel up from the harbour by the island's tractor-drawn toast rack, then on to the farmhouse by horse and carriage.

Please note that the last ferry to Sark departs at 4pm. Full travel guidance will be shared with all confirmed guests to make planning as smooth as possible.
