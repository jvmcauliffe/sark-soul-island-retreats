If switching off were a matter of discipline, you would have done it already. That is the difference between a rule and an environment, and it is why a digital detox on Sark actually holds.

## Why willpower fails and this island works

Ordinary life is engineered to interrupt you. Traffic, notifications, screens in every room, light at every hour. Sark is engineered, by history and by accident, the other way.

There are no cars for visitors, so there is no traffic noise and no rush. There is no street lighting anywhere on the island, so when the sun goes down the night actually arrives, and your body notices. Guests are often surprised by how deeply they sleep from the first night.

## What replaces the phone

The honest answer is that the island out-competes it. Mornings begin with [yoga](/yoga-retreat-sark) and breath work with Monica. The middle of the day is yours: cliff paths above turquoise coves, swims in tidal pools, wildflower lanes, a book in the farmhouse garden.

Meals are long, shared and unhurried, cooked by Bram from what a small island can offer.

## Attention is a resource, and Sark restores it

Psychologists describe what nature does to a tired mind as soft fascination. Cliff horizons, moving water and birds overhead hold your attention gently, without demanding anything, and directed attention slowly recovers.

Sark is bounded by sea in every direction, so that restoration is constant. It is not mystical. It is the mind being given fewer demands and better stimuli. If your exhaustion runs deeper than screen fatigue, our [burnout recovery retreat](/burnout-retreat) speaks to that directly.

## The shape of the week

Five nights at our historic farmhouse with ten to twelve guests. Daily yoga for all levels, guided coastal walks, all meals, and generous free time. Nothing is compulsory and nobody polices your phone. By day three, most guests have simply forgotten where they left it.
