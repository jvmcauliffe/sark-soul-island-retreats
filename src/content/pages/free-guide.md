<p class="lede">The journey to Sark is part of the joy, <em>not a hurdle to clear.</em></p>

Getting to a car-free island takes a little planning: a flight or sailing to Guernsey, the small boat onward, and the famous luggage system that lets you wander Guernsey without dragging a suitcase. None of it is difficult once someone shows you how it fits together. That is what this guide is for.

**Inside the guide:** flights and ferries to Guernsey and how to time them. How the Sark ferry works, a crossing of about 45 minutes. The luggage system, and why your bags arrive without you carrying them. What to pack for the island, including layers for the night sky. And what your first evening on Sark feels like.

It is written by Nadia, who has made the crossing more times than she can count and meets every retreat guest off the boat.

Prefer to read first? The Journal tells the story of [the crossing](/journal/the-crossing) itself, and [Why Sark?](/why-sark) answers the question everyone asks. When you are ready, [dates, rooms and prices for September](/retreats-on-sark) are here.
