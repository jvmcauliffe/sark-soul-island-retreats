Hosting our very first Sark Soul Island Retreat was something I had
dreamed about for a long time.

As the day approached, I felt incredibly excited. Surprisingly, I wasn't
nervous about the retreat itself - my biggest hope was simply that the
weather would be kind to our guests. I wanted them to experience the
island just as I had when I first discovered it, with sunshine, fresh sea
air and that wonderful feeling of leaving the outside world behind.

[photo: the yoga studio set with mats]

The day before everyone arrived was spent preparing every last detail.
The yoga studio was set up with mats, props and a beautiful altar space.
The treatment room was prepared for massages. Each guest room was made
ready with a journal, pen and Turkish beach towel, while the beautiful
old barn was transformed into our dining space, ready for the wonderful
meals our chefs would prepare throughout the week.

[photo: the welcome gifts]

As I worked, I kept imagining our guests making the ferry crossing from
Guernsey. I remembered exactly how I had felt the first time I travelled
to Sark - the excitement of heading towards a tiny island in the middle
of the sea, knowing there were no cars, no traffic and no rush.

The following afternoon, we waited down at the harbour to welcome
everyone. I stood holding a Sark Soul Island Retreats sign, and as the
guests walked through the harbour tunnel we immediately recognised one
another. There were huge smiles, waving, laughter and warm hugs. The
excitement was infectious.

Our adventure began straight away as everyone climbed aboard Sark's
famous toast rack for the journey up Harbour Hill. I remember laughing
and hearing someone call out "hold on to your boobs" because it was going
to be a bumpy ride. Everyone burst into laughter, and from that moment
the atmosphere became relaxed, fun and full of joy.

When our horse and carriage was delayed, nobody minded. We simply stood
together chatting, getting to know one another while we waited. Before
long the carriage arrived and our guests set off on their first tour of
the island, smiling and waving as they disappeared up the lane. I
followed on my bike, looking forward to welcoming everyone properly at
the accommodation.

[photo: a guest bedroom]

That first evening everyone settled into their beautiful rooms before
gathering together for dinner. Our chefs had transformed locally grown
ingredients into colourful vegetarian dishes, and everyone sat around the
large handmade dining table chatting, laughing and enjoying their first
evening together. From that very first meal, it already felt less like
strangers meeting and more like friends sharing an experience.

[photo: morning yoga]

The following morning began with yoga led by Monica. Many of the guests
already knew and loved her teaching, but this was different. Instead of a
weekly class, they were immersed in yoga, meditation and wisdom every
day, surrounded by the peace of Sark. It felt incredibly special.

After breakfast we headed out for our first walk together.

[photo: the goats in the woodland]

As we wandered through the woodland, something changed. The initial
excitement of arriving gently transformed into calm. Everyone naturally
slowed their pace, taking in the birdsong, the trees and the silence.

Then we reached the beach. Anyone who has ever been to Dixcart beach will
recognise the joy of encountering this remarkable place.

[photo: the beach]

As the cove opened before us, I remember hearing people breathe out with
a deep sigh.

There was no need to rush anywhere. Everyone simply spread out naturally.
Some sat quietly looking out to sea. Some paddled in the clear water.
Some explored the rocks. Others journalled or chatted together.

One moment that stayed with me was seeing one of our guests quietly
writing in her journal. She had first visited Sark with her husband
around thirty years earlier, and this was her first visit back after
recently losing him.

Watching her sitting peacefully on the beach was incredibly moving, and I
found myself wondering what memories she was revisiting as she wrote.

We also introduced our group to some of Sark's incredible wild food.
Guests gathered sea lettuce along the shoreline, while I showed them
Pepper Dulse - a seaweed that tastes like black pepper and truffle.
Everyone was fascinated to discover just how much nature provides on this
tiny island.

Each day followed a gentle rhythm of yoga, nourishing food, island
adventures, relaxation and massage.

[photo: the barn at dinner]

The barn quickly became a favourite place for our guests. Each meal
brought laughter, conversation and incredible food prepared by our chefs
using seasonal produce, much of it grown in the gardens around us. I
noticed that nobody ever kept the same seat. People naturally moved
around the table at every meal so everyone had the opportunity to speak
with each other over the course of the week. It created such a lovely
feeling of friendship and connection.

[photo: the treatment room]

The massage treatments also became an important part of the retreat. My
mum and I had the privilege of caring for our guests throughout the week,
and it was wonderful to see people completely relax. Some guests
experienced deep emotional release, while others simply melted into a
state of peace they said they hadn't felt for a long time. Many later
told us that the treatments had been one of the highlights of their stay.

One of the biggest surprises for me was how beautifully the group came
together. Everyone came from different backgrounds with different
personalities, yet there was such kindness, generosity and genuine
interest in one another. As a retreat host, you always hope your guests
will connect, but this group exceeded every expectation.

Throughout the retreat, guests continually shared how much they were
enjoying themselves. They spoke about the yoga, the food, the
accommodation, the island, the walks, the massages and, most of all, the
feeling of peace they had found. Many told us that the retreat had
exceeded their expectations.

By the final day, none of us wanted it to end.

[photo: the group or the coastline]

Saying goodbye was emotional. I felt incredibly proud of everything our
team had created together, but also quite sad that our journey was over.
It honestly felt as though we could all have happily stayed together for
another week.

Looking back now, our first retreat reminded me exactly why I wanted to
create Sark Soul Island Retreats in the first place.

It wasn't simply about yoga.

It wasn't simply about beautiful accommodation or wonderful food.

It was about creating a space where people could slow down, reconnect
with nature, reconnect with themselves and leave feeling lighter than
when they arrived.

Seeing our guests leave with smiling faces, heartfelt hugs and plans to
return next year confirmed that this little island had captured their
hearts just as completely as it had captured mine.
