A Retreat for Burnout, on the Quietest Island in Europe

Not a spa timetable. Not another set of obligations dressed as self-care. Just the conditions in which a tired system can finally stand down.

If you are reading this page, you probably do not need burnout explained to you. The exhaustion that sleep does not fix. The feeling of being permanently switched on and completely empty at the same time. What you need is somewhere the demands actually stop.

Why this island

Burnout is partly a problem of environment

Modern life keeps the body in low-grade vigilance: traffic, noise, screens, light at all hours, the sense of always being reachable and always behind. Sark removes those conditions almost entirely, and it does so without you having to try.

There are no cars for visitors, so the background hum most of us have stopped noticing is simply absent. The island is bounded by sea in every direction, and the horizon becomes a constant, calming anchor. Guests often tell us they feel their shoulders drop within the first day. The island does the work. You just have to arrive.

The programme is rest

A week with a light touch

A retreat for exhausted people should not be strenuous, and this one is not. Mornings begin with gentle yoga and breath work with Monica, taught for all levels and adaptable to however you arrive. Afternoons are unscheduled: walk the cliffs, swim, sleep, read in the farmhouse garden, or genuinely do nothing.

Nothing is compulsory. Rest is not a gap in the programme here. It is the programme.

The sleep

Nights dark enough to actually sleep

There is no street lighting anywhere on Sark. When the sun sets, the island goes dark, the body gets the signal it has been waiting for, and sleep, combined with sea air and days spent walking, arrives the way it was always meant to.

Guests are regularly surprised by how deeply they sleep here from the very first night.

The table

Rest needs company as much as quiet

One of the lonelier parts of burnout is how isolating it is. The retreat is small by design, ten to twelve guests in one historic farmhouse, and something happens around that shared table over five days.

People arrive wound tight and guarded, and leave softer, lighter and connected. You are welcome to as much solitude as you need, but the company is there, and it helps more than people expect.

Frequently asked

I have no energy. Will I keep up?
There is nothing to keep up with. Yoga sessions offer rest and modifications throughout, and every part of the schedule is optional.
Is this a medical or therapeutic programme?
No. We are not a clinic and we do not diagnose or treat. We offer rest, nature, gentle practice and good food in an environment that makes recovery easier. If you are unwell, please speak to your doctor first.
Will I be pushed to join in?
No. Needing an afternoon alone is understood here. Many guests spend their first day mostly sleeping, and that is exactly right.
What do people leave with?
Better sleep, a slower pulse and the feeling of having genuinely stopped. Guests describe arriving wound tight and leaving like a different person.
