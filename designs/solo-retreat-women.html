Coming on your own

A Solo Retreat for Women, on an Island Made for It

Most of our guests arrive alone. It is worth saying that first, because it is the question almost everyone asks and almost no retreat answers plainly.

The island

Why solo travellers choose Sark

Some places are simply easy to be alone in, and Sark is one of them. The island is car-free for visitors, so nowhere feels hurried. The lanes are quiet, the distances are small and everything is reachable on foot or by bicycle.

It is a small community where people greet each other on the paths. On an island with no street lighting, a friendly "evening" on a dark lane is simply how Sark works. None of this needs dramatising. It is just a very comfortable place to travel on your own, which is exactly why so many women do.

The rhythm

Alone, but never lonely

Mornings begin together with yoga, taught by Monica for every level of experience. Meals are shared around one long table, cooked by Bram, and the conversation takes care of itself. Walks along the cliff paths happen in twos and threes and comfortable silences.

The middle of each day is yours. Swim, read in the garden, find a quiet cove, or do nothing at all. Needing an afternoon to yourself is understood here, not questioned.

Day one to day four

Guests who arrive alone leave with friends they keep

The change in the group between the first evening and the fourth is one of the quiet pleasures of hosting these weeks. People arrive as strangers, settle over shared meals and morning practice, and leave exchanging numbers on the ferry home.

You are welcome to as much solitude as you want. The company is simply there when you want it too.

The journey

Getting here is simple

Fly or sail to Guernsey, then take the passenger ferry to Sark, a crossing of around 55 minutes. From the harbour, a tractor-drawn toast rack carries everyone up the hill and a horse and carriage brings you to the retreat house.

Many guests travelling alone find others from the retreat on the same crossing. We help you plan the journey when you book.

Frequently asked

Will I be the only person on my own?
Almost certainly not. Solo guests are the majority on most of our retreats.
Is Sark safe for a woman travelling alone?
Sark is a small, close community with no cars and quiet lanes, and guests are together at the retreat house throughout. Travel from the harbour to the house is arranged for the whole group.
Do I have to share a room?
No. Shared rooms are popular with solo guests and keep the price down, but single rooms are available.
What if I need space from the group?
Free time is built into every day and taking it is completely normal here. The retreat is an invitation, not a timetable.
